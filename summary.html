SUMMARY

Norm Definition Functions Examples
Folkways Social customs or conventions that are considered polite or appropriate within a particular culture, but not necessarily morally significant. Reinforce social cohesion, facilitate smooth interactions, establish social norms, promote social harmony Saying 'please' and 'thank you', dressing modestly in certain settings
Mores Strongly held norms that are considered vital to the well-being of a society, often with moral or ethical implications. Violating mores can lead to severe social sanctions. Maintain moral order, uphold societal values, ensure social stability, discourage harmful behavior Prohibition against theft or murder, respect for elders and authority figures
Laws Formal rules enacted and enforced by a governing authority, typically with legal consequences for non-compliance. Laws regulate behavior and maintain order within a society. Establish legal framework, provide governance structure, resolve disputes, protect rights and freedoms Speed limits on roads, taxation laws, criminal codes
Taboo Social prohibitions or restrictions that are considered sacred, forbidden, or highly offensive within a culture. It often involves strong emotional reactions and may have deep cultural or religious significance. Maintain purity and sanctity, preserve cultural identity, reinforce religious beliefs, deter taboo behavior Incest taboo, eating certain animals considered sacred, speaking ill of the deceased
